About E-Waste

What it is and why it matters.

What is E-Waste?

E-waste, or electronic waste, refers to discarded electrical or electronic devices. This includes everything from old smartphones and laptops to refrigerators and televisions. As technology advances, devices become obsolete faster, leading to a massive increase in e-waste worldwide.

Why is E-Waste a Growing Crisis?

The rapid pace of innovation means we’re replacing gadgets more often. Many of these devices end up in landfills instead of being recycled, releasing toxic substances like lead, mercury, and cadmium into the environment. Only a small fraction of e-waste is properly managed, making it a pressing global issue.

Why Should You Care?

E-waste affects everyone. It pollutes soil and water, harms wildlife, and poses health risks to communities near disposal sites. By understanding e-waste, we can take steps to reduce its impact and build a more sustainable future.
