Dive deeper into the e-waste crisis with facts and links.

Key Facts

  • Global e-waste reached 53.6 million metric tons in 2019, projected to hit 74.7 million by 2030 (Global E-Waste Monitor).
  • Only 17.4% of e-waste is formally collected and recycled, leaving most to landfills or informal processing.
  • E-waste contains valuable materials like gold and copper, worth over $62.5 billion annually if recovered.
  • Improper e-waste disposal releases 98 million tons of CO2-equivalent greenhouse gases yearly.
