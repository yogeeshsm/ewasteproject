Effects of E-Waste

Uncovering the hidden toll of our electronic waste.

Environmental Impact

E-waste dumped in landfills releases toxic chemicals like lead, mercury, and cadmium into the soil and water. These pollutants harm ecosystems, kill wildlife, and contribute to climate change by releasing greenhouse gases during improper disposal. In 2022 alone, over 50 million metric tons of e-waste were generated globally, with only 17% being recycled properly.

Health Risks

Communities near e-waste sites face serious health threats. Exposure to hazardous materials can cause respiratory issues, neurological damage, and even cancer. Informal recycling practices, like burning electronics, release toxic fumes that endanger workers and residents. Studies show that children living near e-waste processing sites have higher rates of developmental disorders.

Social Consequences

The e-waste crisis disproportionately affects poorer regions where waste is shipped for processing. Child labor, low wages, and unsafe working conditions are common in these areas, perpetuating inequality and exploitation in the global waste trade. An estimated 1.8 million children are involved in e-waste recycling, often in hazardous environments.
